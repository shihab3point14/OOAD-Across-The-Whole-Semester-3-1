Sequence diagrams are fundamental tools in Unified Modeling Language (UML) used to model the interaction between objects in a system over time. This report examines the purpose, structure, and practical applications of sequence diagrams in software development. The objective is to understand how sequence diagrams visualize the flow of messages between components, aiding in system design, documentation, and communication among stakeholders. The methodology includes analyzing standard UML notation, constructing sample diagrams, and evaluating their effectiveness in real-world scenarios. Results demonstrate that sequence diagrams effectively clarify complex interactions, identify potential bottlenecks, and improve overall system architecture.
