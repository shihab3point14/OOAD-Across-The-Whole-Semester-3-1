This lab activity recreated agile project management through a hands-on, paper Kanban system to organize team tasks. Over a sequence of nine set activities—from gathering data to performing calculations—participants engaged in visualizing workflow, managing concurrent tasks, assigning roles, and conducting peer review. By arranging sticky notes across four labeled columns (To Do, Doing, Test, Done), the team progressed through tasks, recording every change in status. The activity underscored the value of effective communication, clear division of work, and open processes in team settings. Principal results encompassed the finished and peer-checked completion of all assignments, observed gains in efficiency from parallel work, and noted difficulties involving uniform data and unclear task definitions. This report outlines the approach, outcomes, and lessons learned from applying a straightforward visual coordination tool.
