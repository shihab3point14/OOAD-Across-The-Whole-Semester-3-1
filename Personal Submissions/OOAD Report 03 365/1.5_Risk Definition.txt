Risk Definition
-----------------
A risk is any potential issue that may negatively impact the system’s security, legality, performance, or user trust during development or operation.

1.      ---->Illegal Product Listing Risk<------
        Vendors may attempt to sell illegal products such as drugs, weapons, or restricted items through the platform.
        Impact:
        Legal issues, platform misuse, and damage to system credibility.

2.      ---->Fake or Invalid Payment Risk<------
        Customers may attempt to make payments using fake, invalid, or fraudulent money or payment methods.
        Impact:
        Financial loss and disputes between vendors and customers.

3.      ---->Payment Dispute Risk<------
        Conflicts may arise regarding payment confirmation, delayed payments, or incomplete transactions.
        Impact:
        Customer dissatisfaction and trust issues with the platform.

4.      ---->Product Return and Refund Dispute Risk<------
        Disagreements may occur between vendors and customers regarding product returns, refunds, or damaged goods.
        Impact:
        Increased complaints and operational complexity.

5.      ---->Data Security and Privacy Risk<------
        There is a risk of unauthorized access to vendor or customer data if proper security controls are not implemented.
        Impact:
        Data breaches and loss of user trust.

6.      ---->Vendor Misuse Risk<------
        Some vendors may misuse the platform by providing false product information or misleading customers.
        Impact:
        Reduced reliability and customer confidence.

7.      ---->User Adoption Risk<------
        Vendors may prefer continuing business through social media instead of adopting a new platform.
        Impact:
        Low platform usage and limited success.
8.      ----> Fraudulent Order and Cancellation Risk <----
        Users may place false or spam orders, such as booking Cash on Delivery (COD) items with no intention of accepting delivery, or repeatedly creating and canceling 
        orders to disrupt operations.
        Impact:
        Financial loss due to wasted shipping costs, inventory mismanagement (locking stock from real buyers), and increased operational overhead.
