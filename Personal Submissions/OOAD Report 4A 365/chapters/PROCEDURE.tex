\chapter{PROCEDURE}
\label{ch:procedure}

This chapter describes the step-by-step procedure followed during the laboratory activity. The team collaboratively explored different UML tools, managed tasks using Trello, communicated through Discord, and finally selected Draw.io as the most suitable UML tool.

\section{Phase 1: Tool Assignment and Task Management}

At the beginning of the lab session, five UML tools were assigned to five team members. Each member was responsible for researching one tool and preparing a short report section.

The assigned tools were:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Lucidchart
  \item Draw.io (diagrams.net)
  \item PlantUML
  \item SmartDraw
  \item Creately
\end{itemize}

To manage tasks, the team used Trello with three sections:
\begin{itemize}
  \item To Do
  \item Doing
  \item Testing/Review
\end{itemize}

Team communication was handled through Discord where members shared files, updates, and task assignments.

\section{Phase 2: Tool Research and Exploration}

Each member explored their assigned UML tool by:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Studying features and interface
  \item Creating sample UML diagrams
  \item Identifying advantages and limitations
\end{itemize}

After completing the research, the team compared all tools.

\section{Phase 3: Final Tool Selection}

After discussion and comparison, the team selected \textbf{Draw.io} as the final UML tool.

\textbf{Reasons for choosing Draw.io:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Free and easy to use
  \item Web-based and desktop support
  \item Drag-and-drop interface
  \item Supports all UML diagrams
  \item Easy export and sharing
\end{itemize}

\section{Phase 4: Report Preparation and Submission}

All members completed their report sections. The final report was compiled, reviewed, and submitted with screenshots of Trello and Discord activities as proof of collaboration.
